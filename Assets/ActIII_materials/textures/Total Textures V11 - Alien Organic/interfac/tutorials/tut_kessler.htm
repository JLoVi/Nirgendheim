“The Descendents”

Tutorial by dOUGh

This scene was made using Lightwave for modelling, Photoshop was used for the textures, background image and post work. FPrime was used for final rendering.

Recently I have seen a lot of images dealing with mechanical parts combined with organic elements. I decided to make an image, where a woman is serving as some kind of incubator for an alien race.

The scene is clearly divided into background and foreground. Both were modelled and rendered separately.

Modelling

The mechanical parts are mostly subpatched objects. Only for some simple elements the Lathe Tool was used. Biggest problem was to find the right proportions of the parts compared to each other and the girl. So for every new mechanical part, I made a render of the whole scene and tried out different proportions and angles.

The model of the girl was made point by point as a low-poly object. After the mechanical parts were done, holes were cut into the model of the girl. The edges of the holes got a certain thickness. After the subpatched model was freezed, the edges were further refined. Points were moved to make the edge look irregular.

The model for the background was very bad modelled. I made some spheres and used Boolean to cut the doors in. The mechanical parts were made using polygonal modelling. Subpatching was used for the organic wall – an UV was created for this wall. After this one segment was finished, I cloned it several times to create the whole wall.

Texturing

Since I did not want to do use the model of the girl in anything else than this image, I decided to use Camera Mapping for the girl. It is the fastest way to paint a model for an image. Lightwave’s camera-mapping does not work for bump-maps, so I had to surface-bake the painted bump-map on the color-channel. The bump-map also served as specular-map.

The Texture for the girl was made by composing pinup photos. I hand-painted the wounds and put an overlay texture from the Total Textures v11 on it.

For most of the other objects, planar UV mapping was used. I did not care about stretching problems, since the view of the scene would be strictly from the side. A lot of procedurals were used to make the materials a little rough. For the big flexible tube, the worm in her “stomach” and the liquids, Textures from the CD were directly used with little modifications.

The model for the background got some procedural textures. Only for the organic wall, images from the Total Textures v11 were used for textures.

Rendering

The girl and the tube were rendered first. Then the background was made using the same scene and setup. The Wall was added and placed in the right place. Since it was very hard to find the right angle and place for the wall, I changed the resolution, and made a normal 4:3 format image (the girl was rendered in the wide screen format). That gave me a little room for moving the background a little.

One green area-light was used to light the scene. Radiosity was used for rendering. A bright background served for spending the light. DOF was used for the girl, focus was on her face.

Post-work was made in Photoshop. The background required a lot of painting. The wall was made irregular, by laying over some images from the texture CD. Painted elements, images from different angles of the girl and a lot of textures were used to complete the background.

I needed DOF to separate the foreground from the background. So the background was made a little blurry.

It was hard to get the right look, since I wanted to make the girl part of the machine but stand out very clearly, too.

The Rendered image of the girl got almost no changes in post-work. Only some dark areas were painted on the mechanical parts, to make them blend in with the background.

The most important change was to horizontal flip the finished image. I often hear that this would fool the eye in believing to look at a new image it gives you a new perspective. It works so well…. In fact, I almost hated the final version until I flipped it. That is a nice side-effect. Spending to much time on the same image can give you a bad feeling about it.
